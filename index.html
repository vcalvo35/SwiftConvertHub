Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about unit conversions and how our converters work.

How many kilometers are in a mile?

One mile equals exactly 1.60934 kilometers.

How do you convert Celsius to Fahrenheit?

Multiply the Celsius value by 9/5 and add 32.

What is the difference between GB and GiB?

A gigabyte (GB) equals 1,000,000,000 bytes. A gibibyte (GiB) equals 1,073,741,824 bytes.

How do you convert pounds to kilograms?

Divide the pound value by 2.20462 to get kilograms.

How do you convert a decimal number to binary?

Repeatedly divide by 2 and record remainders from bottom to top.

How many centimeters are in an inch?

Exactly 2.54 centimeters are in one inch — this conversion is exact by international definition since 1959. To convert inches to cm, multiply by 2.54. To go the other way, divide by 2.54 (or multiply by 0.3937). For example: 12 inches = 30.48 cm; 1 meter = 39.3701 inches.

How do I convert Fahrenheit to Celsius?

Subtract 32, then multiply by 5/9. Formula: °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9. Common examples: 32°F = 0°C, 98.6°F = 37°C (body temp), 212°F = 100°C, 350°F = 176.7°C (baking temp). Our temperature converter handles all four scales — Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin, and Rankine.

What is absolute zero in Celsius and Fahrenheit?

Absolute zero — the coldest theoretically possible temperature — is 0 K = −273.15°C = −459.67°F. At this temperature, all molecular motion ceases. It can be approached but never fully reached in practice. The Kelvin scale starts at absolute zero, making it essential for physics, chemistry, and engineering calculations.

How many ounces are in a kilogram?

One kilogram equals 35.274 ounces (avoirdupois). Conversely, one ounce equals approximately 28.3495 grams. Note that "ounce" in cooking can also mean fluid ounce (volume), which is different — a fluid ounce is approximately 29.57 mL. Our weight converter handles kilograms, pounds, ounces, grams, stones, and tonnes.

What is the difference between a megabyte and a mebibyte?

A megabyte (MB) = 1,000,000 bytes in the decimal (SI) system. A mebibyte (MiB) = 1,048,576 bytes in the binary (IEC) system. The difference is about 4.86%. Operating systems like Windows and macOS often report file sizes in binary units (MiB) but label them as "MB," which causes confusion. Our digital storage converter lets you switch between decimal and binary prefixes clearly.

What is hexadecimal used for?

Hexadecimal (base 16) is used extensively in computing as a compact way to represent binary data. Common uses: web colours (#RRGGBB format), memory addresses, MAC addresses, IPv6 addresses, byte-level debugging, and cryptographic hashes. Each hex digit represents exactly 4 binary digits (bits), so 2 hex digits = 1 byte. For example, #FF = 255 decimal = 11111111 binary.

How many feet are in a meter?

One meter equals exactly 3.28084 feet, or equivalently 3 feet and 3.37 inches. The foot is defined as exactly 0.3048 meters. Common reference: a standard doorway is about 2 meters (6.56 ft) tall; an NBA basketball player at 6 ft 6 in is about 1.98 meters tall. Our length converter handles all combinations of feet, inches, meters, and centimeters.

What countries use the metric system?

The metric system (SI) is the official measurement system in every country except the United States, Liberia, and Myanmar. The UK officially uses metric but retains miles for road distances and pints for draught beer. Canada uses metric but miles per hour appear on older infrastructure. Most scientific, medical, and engineering work worldwide uses metric regardless of the country's official system.
