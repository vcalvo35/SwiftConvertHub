How to Convert Pounds to Kilograms (lbs to kg)

📅 2026-03-22  ·  ✏️ Alex Rivera
Last updated: April 2026  ·  Accuracy note

The Pounds to Kilograms Formula

The pound (lb) is the primary unit of weight in the imperial system, while the kilogram (kg) is the base unit of mass in the metric system. The exact conversion is:

1 pound = 0.453592 kilograms

To convert pounds to kg: multiply by 0.453592. To convert kg to pounds: multiply by 2.20462.

  • 50 lbs = 22.68 kg
  • 100 lbs = 45.36 kg
  • 150 lbs = 68.04 kg
  • 200 lbs = 90.72 kg

Common Use Cases

Body weight: If you weigh 154 lbs, that's 70 kg. If someone says they weigh 80 kg, that's about 176 lbs.

Gym and fitness: A 45-lb barbell plate is about 20.4 kg. A 25-lb dumbbell is roughly 11.3 kg.

Cooking and shipping: Recipe ingredients or package weights are often listed in one system and need converting for international use.

A Quick Mental Shortcut

To roughly convert kg to pounds in your head, multiply by 2 and add 10%. So 70 kg × 2 = 140, plus 10% (14) = 154 lbs. This gives a result within about 0.2% of the exact answer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the exact pounds to kilograms conversion factor?

The exact factor is 1 pound = 0.45359237 kilograms, or equivalently 1 kg = 2.20462262185 lbs. This exact value was set by international agreement in 1959. For everyday use, 1 lb ≈ 0.454 kg and 1 kg ≈ 2.205 lbs are accurate enough.

How do I convert my body weight from pounds to kg?

Divide your weight in pounds by 2.205. For example: 160 lbs ÷ 2.205 = 72.6 kg. Or use our Weight Converter above for an instant, precise result. Medical and fitness contexts worldwide use kilograms as the standard.

What is a stone in pounds and kilograms?

One stone (used mainly in the UK and Ireland for body weight) equals 14 pounds or approximately 6.35 kilograms. So 11 stone would be 154 lbs or about 69.9 kg. Our weight converter supports stones alongside all other common units.
