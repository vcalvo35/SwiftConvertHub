Grams to Ounces in Baking: Precision Conversions for Better Recipes

Why Grams Beat Ounces in Baking

Baking is chemistry. Unlike cooking, where a "pinch of salt" and "splash of olive oil" work fine, baking requires accurate proportions of flour, sugar, fat, and leavening to produce consistent results. That's why professional bakers and serious home bakers worldwide prefer weighing ingredients in grams rather than measuring by volume. The reason is simple: volume measurements lie. A "cup" of all-purpose flour can weigh anywhere from 120 g (lightly spooned) to 160 g (packed) — a 33% difference that will completely change your cake's texture. A gram is a gram.

However, many classic American baking recipes use ounces and pounds, and many online recipes mix grams and ounces freely. This guide gives you the exact conversion formula, a comprehensive ingredient reference table, and practical tips for scaling recipes accurately.

The Exact Grams-to-Ounces Formula

The conversion between grams and avoirdupois ounces is based on the exact definition of the pound: 1 pound = 0.45359237 kg exactly, and 1 pound = 16 ounces. Therefore:

  • 1 ounce = 28.3495 grams (exact: 0.45359237 kg ÷ 16 × 1000)
  • 1 gram = 0.035274 ounces

To convert grams to ounces: divide by 28.3495 (or multiply by 0.035274)
To convert ounces to grams: multiply by 28.3495

For quick mental math in the kitchen: divide grams by 28 for an approximate ounce result (slightly high but close enough for most purposes). For precise baking, use our weight converter above.

Scaling Baking Recipes: Doubling and Halving

When scaling a recipe, weight measurements scale perfectly — simply double or halve the gram/ounce values. But leavening agents (baking powder, baking soda, yeast) don't always scale linearly. A common guideline: for recipes above 4× the original size, use only 75% of the proportional leavening amount. Pan size also matters — doubling a recipe doesn't double the baking time, because the batter is now in a wider, shallower pan.

The Case for a Kitchen Scale

A digital kitchen scale that reads in both grams and ounces is the most accurate baking tool you can own. Scales cost under $15 and eliminate the need for cups, spoons, and conversion tables for most baking. Professional pastry chefs weigh everything, including liquids (1 ml of water = 1 g at 20°C, so 250 ml of water = 250 g). For home baking, even a basic 0.1 g precision scale transforms recipe consistency.
