GB vs TB: How Much Storage Do You Really Need?

📅 2026-03-19  ·  ✏️ Victor A. Calvo S.
Last updated: April 2026  ·  Accuracy note

GB vs TB: The Basic Difference

A gigabyte (GB) is 1,024 megabytes. A terabyte (TB) is 1,024 gigabytes — meaning 1 TB is roughly 1,000 times larger than 1 GB. Understanding this difference is essential when buying storage for phones, computers, cameras, or cloud services.

What Fits in 1 GB?

  • ~230 MP3 songs (at 128 kbps)
  • ~500 high-quality photos (from a smartphone)
  • ~1–2 hours of standard-definition video
  • ~250 PDF documents

What Fits in 1 TB?

  • ~230,000 MP3 songs
  • ~500,000 high-quality photos
  • ~250 hours of 4K video
  • ~1,000 hours of HD video

Storage Recommendations by Use Case

  • Casual smartphone user: 64–128 GB is usually enough
  • Power smartphone user / photographer: 256–512 GB
  • Laptop for work and light media: 256 GB–1 TB SSD
  • Video editor / content creator: 2–4 TB (or more)
  • Home server / NAS: 4–20 TB

Cloud Storage Reference

  • Google Photos free tier: 15 GB
  • iCloud basic plan: 5 GB (free) or 50 GB ($0.99/mo)
  • Dropbox Plus: 2 TB
  • Google One: up to 30 TB available

Step-by-Step Conversion Examples

How Many 4K Movies Fit on a 2 TB Drive?

A 4K HDR movie encoded in H.265 (HEVC) at high quality is typically 50–80 GB. Using 60 GB as a realistic average:

2,000 GB ÷ 60 GB per movie = ~33 movies (using the decimal TB label)

However, the operating system reports space in GiB. 2 TB = 1,862.6 GiB in binary. After 10–15% OS overhead and filesystem metadata, practical usable space is roughly 1,600–1,700 GiB. At ~56 GiB per film: roughly 28–30 films on real usable space. A useful rule: plan for 80% maximum utilization — drives slow down when nearly full, and you need headroom for temp files during encoding and editing.

Planning Storage for a Photography Business

A professional photographer shoots 2,000 RAW images per month. A RAW file from a 45-megapixel camera is approximately 45 MB. Annual storage calculation:

  • Monthly: 2,000 × 45 MB = 90,000 MB = 90 GB per month
  • Annual (RAW originals): 90 GB × 12 = 1,080 GB ≈ 1.08 TB
  • With JPEG exports at 8 MB each: +192 GB/year
  • With full-resolution TIFF edits at 130 MB each: +3.1 TB/year

Professional best practice: maintain 3 copies — primary working SSD (2 TB), local backup drive (4 TB), and offsite/cloud backup (Amazon S3 Glacier or Backblaze B2 at ~$6/TB/month). The cost of cloud backup for 1 TB is approximately $6/month — less than the value of a single lost client shoot.

Choosing Between 256 GB, 512 GB, and 1 TB SSD for a Laptop

A 256 GB NVMe SSD has these real-world constraints after setup:

  • Windows 11 installation: ~20 GB
  • Office Suite + common apps: ~15 GB
  • Browser cache + downloads folder: ~10 GB
  • Remaining for files: ~211 GB ≈ 196 GiB usable

For typical office work, 256 GB is adequate. For developers, 512 GB is more practical — a Node.js project with node_modules can consume 2–5 GB; a Docker setup with several images: 20–80 GB. For video editors or machine learning practitioners, 1 TB is a minimum starting point with external storage for project archives. SSD performance also degrades when more than 80% full because there's less space for TRIM optimization and wear leveling.

Understanding Cloud Storage Quotas in Practice

Google's free 15 GB quota and what it means for different file types:

  • 15 GB ÷ 3 MB per email with attachment ≈ ~5,000 emails
  • 15 GB ÷ 4 MB per smartphone photo ≈ ~3,750 photos
  • 15 GB ÷ 8 MB per minute of 1080p video ≈ ~31 minutes of video
  • Google Docs, Sheets, Slides: do not count toward quota (as of 2026)

Google Photos originally offered unlimited compressed photo storage for free until June 2021. Photos uploaded before that date still don't count against your quota — only those uploaded after do. If you have a Google account with tens of thousands of pre-2021 photos, your available 15 GB may appear surprisingly unaffected even with a large library.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many GB are in 1 TB?

In decimal terms (as used by hard drive manufacturers), 1 TB = 1,000 GB. In binary terms (as reported by operating systems), 1 TiB = 1,024 GiB. This is why a 1 TB hard drive shows approximately 931 GiB in Windows. For casual storage questions, the answer is 1 TB ≈ 1,000 GB.

How much storage do I need for 1,000 photos?

It depends on the camera. A smartphone photo from a modern iPhone or Android is typically 3–8 MB. So 1,000 photos ≈ 3–8 GB. RAW photos from a DSLR can be 20–50 MB each, meaning 1,000 RAW files = 20–50 GB. Always aim for at least 2× your estimated needs to leave room for growth.

Is 256 GB enough for a laptop in 2026?

256 GB is adequate for light use (documents, email, web browsing) but tight for video editing, gaming, or storing a large photo library. Most power users find 512 GB more comfortable as a minimum. If cloud storage is part of your workflow, 256 GB can work longer-term for everyday tasks.
