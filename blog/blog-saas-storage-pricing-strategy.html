If you offer a cloud storage service — or any digital product where space is a resource to sell — there is a technical error that can generate claims and cancellations: confusing GB with GiB. They are not the same, and the difference is not small.

Si ofreces un servicio de almacenamiento en la nube — o cualquier producto digital donde el espacio sea un recurso a vender — hay un error técnico que puede generarte reclamos y cancelaciones: confundir GB con GiB. No son lo mismo, y la diferencia no es pequeña.

GB vs GiB: The Difference You Must Understand Before Setting PricesGB vs GiB: La Diferencia que Debes Entender Antes de Poner Precios

A gigabyte (GB) in the decimal system equals 1,000,000,000 bytes. A gibibyte (GiB) in the binary IEC system equals 1,073,741,824 bytes. The difference is approximately 7.4%. If you promise "10 GiB" using the "10 GB" label when your system uses the decimal standard, the client receives less space than expected and has a right to complain.

Un gigabyte (GB) en el sistema decimal equivale a 1,000,000,000 bytes. Un gibibyte (GiB) en el sistema binario IEC equivale a 1,073,741,824 bytes. La diferencia es de casi el 7.4%. Si prometes "10 GiB" usando la etiqueta "10 GB" cuando tu sistema usa el estándar decimal, el cliente recibe menos espacio del que esperaba y tiene razón al quejarse.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should SaaS pricing use GB or GiB?

Most SaaS companies use decimal gigabytes (GB = 1,000,000,000 bytes) for marketing purposes because users expect this convention and it's how hard drive manufacturers advertise. However, underlying cloud infrastructure (AWS, GCP, Azure) often bills in binary units. Document which standard you use clearly to avoid customer confusion.

How does the GB vs GiB difference affect storage plan revenue?

The difference is about 7.4% at the gigabyte level (1 GiB = 1.074 GB). If a customer buys a '100 GB' plan but your system provisions in GiB, they actually get about 107 GB of raw storage. At scale, this gap materially affects infrastructure costs and must be accounted for in pricing models.
