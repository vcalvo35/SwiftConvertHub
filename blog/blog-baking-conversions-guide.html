The Complete Baking Conversion Guide: Cups, Grams, Tablespoons & Milliliters

📅 2026-05-21  ·  ✏️ Victor A. Calvo S.
Last updated: May 2026  ·  Accuracy note

Baking is precise. A recipe that calls for “1 cup of flour” can mean anywhere from 120 g to 160 g depending on how you scoop, which is a 33% difference that directly affects texture. This guide covers every baking conversion you’ll need — volume to weight, cups to milliliters, and oven temperatures — so your results are consistent every time.

Volume Measurements: Cups, Tablespoons, and Teaspoons

US recipes use volumetric measurements. Here are the exact relationships:

Note on Australian tablespoons: Australian recipes use a 20 mL tablespoon (4 teaspoons), not 15 mL. If you’re following an Australian recipe, 1 tbsp = 1⅓ US tablespoons — watch out for leavening agents where a small difference matters.

Cups to Grams: Common Baking Ingredients

Cups measure volume; grams measure mass. Because ingredients have different densities, there is no single “cups to grams” conversion — it depends on the ingredient. Below are standard values used by professional bakers:

Oven Temperature Conversions

European recipes use Celsius; US recipes use Fahrenheit. Fan-assisted ovens (convection) run hotter — reduce the stated temperature by 20°C (35°F) when your oven has a fan.

Why Weighing is Better Than Measuring by Cup

A cup of flour can weigh anywhere from 100 g (very lightly spooned) to 165 g (scooped directly from the bag). That 65 g difference is over 50% — enough to turn a tender cake dense and dry. Professional bakers always weigh ingredients in grams or ounces because a kitchen scale eliminates this variability entirely.

If you’re switching to weight measurements, investing in a digital kitchen scale (accuracy to 1 g) is the single best upgrade for consistent baking results.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many grams is 1 cup of flour?

1 cup of all-purpose flour = approximately 120–130 g using the spoon-and-level method. Scooping directly from the bag can push it to 150–160 g. For consistent results, weigh flour in grams rather than measuring by volume.

How many tablespoons are in a cup?

1 US cup = 16 tablespoons = 48 teaspoons = 236.6 mL. So 1/4 cup = 4 tbsp, 1/3 cup ≈ 5 tbsp + 1 tsp, and 1/2 cup = 8 tbsp. Australian cups are 250 mL (slightly larger).

What is 180°C in Fahrenheit?

180°C = 356°F, conventionally rounded to 350°F in US recipes. This is the most common baking temperature for cakes and muffins. For fan-assisted ovens, reduce by 20°C to 160°C (320°F).

Is 1 tablespoon the same everywhere?

No. A US tablespoon = 14.8 mL (3 teaspoons). An Australian tablespoon = 20 mL (4 teaspoons). When following Australian recipes, adjust tablespoon measurements of leavening agents accordingly.
